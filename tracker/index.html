There are three levels of probation. If you exceed three levels, you will be dismissed from NHS. Below you will find the number of probation levels imposed for specific infractions.

One Level

  • Failure to answer an email sent by an NHS Officer, Mr. DeSilva, or another faculty member.
  • Failure to sign up for a mandatory project before a deadline.
  • Failure to attend a project for which you volunteered without notifying an NHS Officer or Mr. DeSilva in advance.
  • 2 excused absences from meetings (each additional absence beyond this equals one level).
  • Submitting a late absence note. Please note that absence notes received after the next meeting will not be considered.

Two Levels

  • Not attending a mandatory project (unless you have been cleared by the officers)
  • One unexcused absence. If you have two, you will be dismissed.

Removing Levels of Probation

To be removed from one level of probation, you may complete an extra project after you have completed your other project requirements.

Example 1: An NHS member decides to skip an NHS meeting without submitting an absence note and is assigned two levels of probation. Later in the semester, the same NHS member completes two additional projects and is removed from probation.

Example 2: An NHS member misses two meetings (excused absences) and is given one level of probation. The following week, they fail to attend a mandatory project and are given two additional levels of probation. If they get one more level of probation, they will be dismissed from NHS.

Example 3: An NHS member decides to skip a project for which they volunteered and is given one level of probation. The following week, they decide to skip a meeting without providing an absence note and receive two additional levels of probation. Finally, they fail to sign up for a mandatory project before a given deadline and are dismissed from NHS because they have exceeded three levels of probation.

Stay on top of your responsibilities and don't let this happen to you!

  • Excused Absence (per semester): You are allowed one excused absence per semester. After that, any more excused absences will give you one level of probation.
  • Unexcused Absence (per semester): Your first unexcused absence will give you two levels of probation. After that, any more unexcused absences will result in dismissal from NHS.
  • Late Absence Note: If you submit an absence note late, you will receive 1 level of probation. Any absence notes received after the next NHS meeting will not be considered. Submit a picture or scanned copy of your absence notes at this link. No paper absence notes will be accepted.
