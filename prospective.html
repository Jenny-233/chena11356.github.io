NHS Application Process

For Underclassmen

Congratulations for thinking about NHS so early in your high school career! Now is the perfect time to start accumulating credits. You may use the links below to back up your credit sheets and verification letters, as well as record credits in advance of applying to NHS.

Before reading the instructions below, please carefully note that you are responsible for retaining the paper copies of your own credit sheets and verification letters. We strongly encourage that you make backups of your own documents as well. If materials are incomplete or improperly submitted after the application deadline, they will not be considered.

Any prospective applicant that is caught forging a teacher's signature will be exempt from applying to NHS and subject to disciplinary action.

As an underclassman, you have the option of using the Underclassman Pre-Application to keep track of your credits so that you are ready to apply as a junior or senior. Alternatively, you may also submit underclassman activity forms here—if so, please follow the instructions outlined here. These will be visible on your application as soon as they are processed. If you have already uploaded these forms previously, then they will be added to your application file as soon as possible.

No matter your method of keeping track of your activities, out-of-school activity verification letters should be submitted here. Please follow the instructions outlined here.

At the end of each year, your information will automatically roll over. For example, if you start your pre-application as a freshman, your information will transfer to sophomore year, and if you continue your pre-application as a sophomore, your information will then transfer to junior year as well. We only review final applications for juniors and seniors; the pre-application is for your own convenience and will not affect your chances of admission into NHS.

If you have any questions, please read the FAQ below. If you still have questions, please email nhs@bxscience.edu.

For Juniors

We're excited that you're thinking about joining NHS! As a junior in NHS, you will be eligible to apply for an NHS board position in May and you will have more opportunities to volunteer at events like the Induction Ceremony and Graduation.

Before reading the instructions below, please carefully note that you are responsible for retaining the paper copies of your own credit sheets and verification letters. We strongly encourage that you make backups of your own documents as well. If materials are incomplete or improperly submitted after the application deadline, they will not be considered.

Any prospective applicant that is caught forging a teacher's signature will be exempt from applying to NHS and subject to disciplinary action.

The application has three main components: the electronic application, the paper application, and out-of-school verification letters (optional).

You have the option of using the Upperclassman Application to keep track of your credits and start or work on your electronic application. Alternatively, you may submit your application all at once using this Google Form. No matter which method you use, your application should be complete or submitted by the application deadline. Incomplete applications will not be considered. If you fill out the web app application, you do not need to fill out the Google Form, and vice versa.

You will also need to submit a scanned paper copy of the application for verification of hours. Your paper application should be identical to your electronic application, and signed by the appropriate faculty members. Once you are ready, follow the uploading instructions here and submit your paper application here.

Out-of-school activity verification letters should be submitted here. Please follow the instructions outlined here. Letters must be written on the letterhead of the organization for which you volunteered and signed by the adult that supervised your participation.

If you have any questions, please read the FAQ below. If you still have questions, please email nhs@bxscience.edu.

Requirements

Applying as a junior who entered Bronx Science as a freshman:
  • Scholarship: You must have a 90 or above cumulative average.
  • Service: You must have 13 credits.
  • Leadership and Citizenship: You must have 50 credits total (any combination of credits from either or both categories).
Applying as a junior who entered Bronx Science as a sophomore:
  • Scholarship: You must have a 90 or above cumulative average.
  • Service: You must have 8 credits.
  • Leadership and Citizenship: You must have 30 credits total (any combination of credits from either or both categories).

For example, you can get service credit for volunteering at the Kids Walk for Kids With Cancer, even though you went with the Cancer Society, because the event was not mandatory. You cannot get credit for participating in Big Bronx as a member of the Speech & Debate team, since it is a mandatory event for all debaters.
Since the yearbook and newspaper are classes, you may not receive credit for being a member of either. You may, however, receive credit for an editor position.

New York Council for the Humanities

Community Conversations provide arenas in which people come together for thoughtful discussion and dialogue about shared values as Americans—past, present, and future. Focused on central themes in American life such as service, freedom, and democracy, Community Conversations allows New Yorkers to join in discussions that offer an alternative to received wisdom and provide an opportunity to take part in a shared national dialogue.

Community Conversations for Young Adults engages middle school, high school, or college students in a facilitated discussion of a short reading in or out of school.

Gilda's Club New York City

The Teen Program at Gilda’s Club NYC helps individuals impacted by cancer-men, women, teens and children-along with their families and friends.Their innovative program is an essential complement to medical care, providing networking and support groups, workshops, lectures and social activities, all free of charge.

Teens Connect program provides support to teens impacted by cancer in any way. It is open to teens that are living with a personal cancer diagnosis, have a friend or loved one living with cancer, or have lost a friend or family member to cancer.
