Welcome to the Bronx Science National Honor Society!

Gaining membership to the National Honor Society (NHS) is one of the greatest academic honors that a student may receive at Bronx Science. In order to be accepted as a member, students are required to meet rigorous academic and service requirements. Each member must possess exceptional levels of scholarship, service, leadership, and character in order to meet this goal. Information about the overarching organization can be found here.

Information for Prospective NHS Applicants

Applications to the National Honor Society are accepted twice a year: Seniors apply during fall semester and Juniors apply during spring semester. Applications will be completed through this web app. Application deadlines will be announced in advance and publicized widely throughout Bronx Science. For information about the application process and requirements, please click here. To start or complete an application, please click here.

Once you've submitted an application, you will receive notification of your admission status shortly after the application deadline. If you are accepted to the National Honor Society, your guidance counselor will receive an official notification of your membership.

Tutoring

Part of the responsibilities of NHS include tutoring individuals who may want extra help with a subject. Please use the appropriate google forms (links provided on right) to submit all tutoring requests. To document tutoring hours, use this form.

Office Hours Tutoring: NHS Members will be available throughout the day in the library multipurpose room and the guidance office.

Note: To access any google sign up forms, you must be logged in to your Bronx Science account. Emails sent to Mr. DeSilva or any NHS Officer requesting to share Google forms will not receive a reply.

Any tutoring ideas or questions should be directed to Margaret Wong (National Honor Society Vice President of Tutoring) at wongm@bxscience.edu.

Please use this form to request a tutor. Margaret Wong (National Honor Society Vice President of Tutoring) will match tutors and tutees according to the subject. If you do not hear back from Margaret within a few days, please email her directly at wongm@bxscience.edu.
Teachers may request a National Honor Society tutor for their students by submitting this form or to assist them during SGI by submitting this form.
  1. How do I request a tutor?
    Fill out the Tutoring Request form and you will be matched with a NHS tutor who can tutor in that subject and has the same free periods as you.

  2. I requested a tutor but haven’t heard back yet, what do I do?
    Please be patient. If it has been more than a week, and you still don't have a tutor, then you may email Samita Rahaman (VP of Tutoring) to inquire about your request.

  3. I am a parent. How do I sign my child up for a tutor?
    Have your child fill out the Tutoring Request form (there is no paper form) and he/she will be matched with a NHS tutor who can tutor in the requested subject. Students requesting a tutor must use their Bronx Science email address.

  4. Can I meet with my tutor outside of school?
    Unfortunately, no. All tutoring must be done in school, in a space with teacher supervision such as the library or a classroom during SGI.

  5. Will tutoring save my grade?
    We hope it will, but ultimately, our tutors are not professionals. We recommend going to SGI, using resources such as review books or KhanAcademy, or seeking additional outside help if you are really struggling.
  1. I am an NHS member who signed up to become a tutor, but my tutee is not responding. What do I do?
    Email them again, and be sure to Cc Samita. It is your responsibility to keep track of your tutoring assignments. If the student still doesn't respond, please notify Samita and she will inform Mr. DeSilva, who will then take further action to contact the student.

  2. How many tutoring hours can I do outside of school?
    You must do at least ten tutoring hours in school. The other ten can be from outside tutoring. Or, you could do five hours of outside tutoring and one additional project.

  3. I am a student who would like to tutor for NHS, even though I am not a member. How can I register?
    Fill out the Underclassmen Tutoring Registration Form. Then, ask a teacher to verify that you are qualified to tutor, using this form. If you would like to get credit for joining NHS in the future, just download and print out the NHS Tutoring Form. Also, although you may receive up to two assignments, please realize that you may not get an assignment right away.

Uploading Instructions

Please upload out-of-school activity documentation (applicants and members), tutoring forms (members), and NHS Contracts (members) only!

  1. Scan your file in pdf format and save as "your last name, your first name." For example, "DeSilva, Paul." Do not include the quotation marks.
  2. Please be sure that cookies are enabled in your browser’s preferences.
  3. If uploading out-of-school verification letters, click here. If uploading absence notes, click here.
  4. Attach a single file.
  5. Fill in first name, last name, and email address.
  6. Click the “Upload” button.
  7. You will receive a confirmation email (provided that you supplied the correct email address) from Dropbox, indicating that your files have been uploaded successfully.
  8. Repeat steps 1-7 for each individual file.
